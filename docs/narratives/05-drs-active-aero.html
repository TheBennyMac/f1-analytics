DRS vs Active Aero

Did DRS make overtaking artificial — and does 2026 show a difference?

The narrative

“DRS made overtaking artificial — it turned genuine racing moves into highway passes where the chasing driver simply drove past on the straight. Removing it should restore real racing.”

The hypothesis predicts that overtaking activity should be lower without DRS than with it. The 2026 regulation change — replacing DRS with active aerodynamics — provides a natural experiment, comparing Ground Effect Era (DRS on, 2022–2025) against 2026 (active aero).

Method

The overtake index is computed for every race across both eras and compared. Because 2026 has only 3 completed rounds at the time of writing, this comparison is directional only.

from src.utils.era_helper import get_era_name
combined_oi['era'] = combined_oi['season'].apply(get_era_name)

ERA_COLOURS = {
    'Ground Effect Era': '#636EFA',
    '2026 Era': '#FF6B35',
}

fig = go.Figure()

for era, colour in ERA_COLOURS.items():
    sub = combined_oi[combined_oi['era'] == era]
    fig.add_trace(go.Scatter(
        x=sub['season'],
        y=sub['overtake_index'],
        mode='markers',
        name=era,
        marker=dict(color=colour, size=8, opacity=0.8),
        customdata=sub[['race_name', 'season', 'round', 'positions_gained', 'starters']].values,
        hovertemplate=(
            '<b>%{customdata[0]}</b><br>'
            '%{customdata[1]} R%{customdata[2]}<br>'
            'Overtake index: %{y:.3f}<br>'
            'Positions gained: %{customdata[3]} / %{customdata[4]} starters'
            '<extra></extra>'
        ),
    ))

# Era mean lines — annotations offset vertically so they don't overlap
annotation_yshifts = {'Ground Effect Era': 10, '2026 Era': -16}
for era, colour in ERA_COLOURS.items():
    sub = combined_oi[combined_oi['era'] == era]
    mean_oi = sub['overtake_index'].mean()
    x_min = sub['season'].min()
    x_max = sub['season'].max()
    fig.add_shape(
        type='line',
        x0=x_min - 0.3, x1=x_max + 0.3,
        y0=mean_oi, y1=mean_oi,
        line=dict(color=colour, width=2, dash='dash'),
    )
    fig.add_annotation(
        x=x_max + 0.3, y=mean_oi,
        text=f'{era} mean: {mean_oi:.3f}',
        showarrow=False,
        xanchor='left',
        font=dict(color=colour, size=11),
        yshift=annotation_yshifts[era],
    )

all_seasons = sorted(combined_oi['season'].unique())
fig.update_layout(
    title='Overtake Index per Race — Ground Effect Era vs 2026 (Active Aero)',
    xaxis=dict(
        title='Season',
        tickvals=all_seasons,
        ticktext=[str(s) for s in all_seasons],
    ),
    yaxis_title='Overtake index (positions gained / starters)',
    template='plotly_dark',
    height=500,
    margin=dict(t=60, r=160, b=100),
    legend=dict(
        orientation='h',
        yanchor='top',
        y=-0.15,
        xanchor='center',
        x=0.5,
    ),
)
fig.show()

Findings

Question Finding
Mean overtake index — Ground Effect Era (DRS, 2022–2025)? 1.688 across 92 races
Mean overtake index — 2026 Era (active aero, 3 rounds)? 1.874 across 3 rounds
Does removing DRS appear to reduce position changes? No — 2026 shows a higher index
Is the “DRS made overtaking artificial” narrative supported? Not supported

Verdict: Not supported — on 3 rounds of data.

If DRS were artificially inflating overtaking, its removal should produce fewer position changes. Instead the opposite is observed: early 2026 races show a higher overtake index than the DRS era average.

Two important caveats apply. First, 3 rounds is a very small sample — the 2026 figure is directional only and must be revisited as the season matures. Second, the early 2026 calendar (Australia, China, Japan) may skew the comparison — these tracks produce different overtaking profiles to the full calendar average.

The data currently challenges the narrative, but a full season is needed before drawing firm conclusions.
