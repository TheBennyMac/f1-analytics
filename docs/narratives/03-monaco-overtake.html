Monaco Overtake Index

Is Monaco uniquely processional, or just one of several qualifying spectacles?

The narrative

“Monaco produces processional races — it is uniquely unsuited to overtaking and should be dropped from the calendar.”

The hypothesis predicts Monaco would rank as the lowest-overtaking circuit on the calendar by a clear margin.

Method

Overtake index = total positions gained by classified finishers ÷ starters per race. This normalises for field size and attrition. DNFs and pit-lane starters (grid position = 0) are excluded — their position changes do not reflect on-track overtaking.

Data covers 2022–2025 (92 races, 22 circuits).

Findings

Question Finding
Where does Monaco rank among all circuits? 2nd lowest — behind Japan
How does Monaco compare to the field mean? Considerably lower
Are there circuits similarly processional to Monaco? Japan is the only comparable one
Is the narrative supported? Partially

Verdict: Partially supported.

Monaco is genuinely one of the least overtaking circuits on the calendar — but it is not uniquely so. The Japanese Grand Prix ranks below Monaco on mean overtake index and yet attracts little of the same criticism. Both circuits are qualifying spectacles: because grid position is structurally decisive on street and high-downforce tracks, the races reward Saturday performance rather than Sunday wheel-to-wheel racing.

The narrative holds in the sense that Monaco produces fewer overtakes than a typical round — but the “uniquely processional” framing does not survive scrutiny. Japan is a parallel case that the received wisdom tends to ignore.
