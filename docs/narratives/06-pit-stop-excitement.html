Pit Stop Excitement

Are races more competitive before the pit window closes?

The narrative

“Races are more exciting before the pit stop — the first half of a Grand Prix, with cars on original tyres and natural degradation, produces closer racing than the post-stop phase, where fresh tyres spread the field.”

The hypothesis predicts the pre-pit-stop phase shows more position change activity per lap than the post-stop phase.

Method

For each full-length Grand Prix (sprint weekends excluded), the median first pit lap across all drivers is used as the phase boundary. Position changes per lap are computed separately for the pre- and post-stop phases.

Seasons analysed: 2022 (17 non-sprint races) and 2026 (2 non-sprint races: Australia and Japan — China was a sprint weekend).

import sys
sys.path.insert(0, '../..')

import pandas as pd
import plotly.graph_objects as go
import scripts.export_data as exp

excitement = exp.read('pit_excitement_by_race')

era_summary = excitement.groupby('era').agg(
    races=('pre_positions_gained_per_lap', 'count'),
    mean_pre=('pre_positions_gained_per_lap', 'mean'),
    mean_post=('post_positions_gained_per_lap', 'mean'),
).reset_index().round(3)

print(era_summary.to_string(index=False))

results_df = exp.read('results_2022_2025')
results_2026 = exp.read('results_2026')
race_names_2022 = (
    results_df[['season', 'round', 'race_name']]
    .drop_duplicates()
)
race_names_2026 = (
    results_2026[['season', 'round', 'race_name']]
    .drop_duplicates()
)
race_names = pd.concat([race_names_2022, race_names_2026])

plot_df = excitement.merge(race_names, on=['season', 'round'], how='left')

ERA_COLOURS = {'Ground Effect Era (2022)': '#636EFA', '2026 Era': '#FF6B35'}

fig = go.Figure()

for era, colour in ERA_COLOURS.items():
    sub = plot_df[plot_df['era'] == era]
    fig.add_trace(go.Scatter(
        x=sub['pre_positions_gained_per_lap'],
        y=sub['post_positions_gained_per_lap'],
        mode='markers',
        name=era,
        marker=dict(color=colour, size=10, opacity=0.85),
        customdata=sub[['race_name', 'season', 'round', 'split_lap']].values,
        hovertemplate=(
            '<b>%{customdata[0]}</b><br>'
            '%{customdata[1]} R%{customdata[2]}<br>'
            'Split lap: %{customdata[3]:.0f}<br>'
            'Pre-stop: %{x:.3f} pos/lap<br>'
            'Post-stop: %{y:.3f} pos/lap<extra></extra>'
        ),
    ))

# Diagonal parity line (pre = post)
max_val = max(
    plot_df['pre_positions_gained_per_lap'].max(),
    plot_df['post_positions_gained_per_lap'].max(),
) * 1.05
fig.add_shape(
    type='line', x0=0, y0=0, x1=max_val, y1=max_val,
    line=dict(color='white', dash='dash', width=1),
)
fig.add_annotation(
    x=max_val * 0.7, y=max_val * 0.7,
    text='pre = post', showarrow=False,
    font=dict(color='white', size=10), opacity=0.5,
    textangle=-45,
)

fig.update_layout(
    title='Pre vs Post Pit Stop Position Changes per Lap',
    xaxis_title='Pre-stop: positions gained per lap',
    yaxis_title='Post-stop: positions gained per lap',
    template='plotly_dark',
    height=480,
    legend=dict(orientation='h', yanchor='bottom', y=1.02, xanchor='right', x=1),
)
fig.show()

Findings

Question Finding
Mean positions gained per lap — pre pit stop (2022)? 2.592 across 17 races
Mean positions gained per lap — post pit stop (2022)? 2.556 — virtually identical
Mean positions gained per lap — pre pit stop (2026)? 4.052 across 2 races
Mean positions gained per lap — post pit stop (2026)? 1.144 — a dramatic drop
Is pre-stop racing more active than post-stop? In 2026 yes; in 2022 negligibly
Is the pattern consistent across both eras? No — era-specific

Verdict: Partially supported — but era-specific.

In 2022 the pre/post split is negligible (2.592 vs 2.556 positions per lap) — the narrative does not hold for the Ground Effect Era. Racing was equally competitive in both phases.

In 2026 the effect is dramatic: pre-stop racing produces 3.5× more position changes per lap than the post-stop phase (4.052 vs 1.144). Points above the diagonal line on the chart would suggest post-stop is more active — instead all 2026 races fall well below it.

This may reflect 2026 tyre characteristics — the Hard compound appears to produce very processional post-stop racing. However, only 2 non-sprint 2026 races are in the sample (Australia and Japan). This finding is directional only and must be revisited as Miami and subsequent rounds are added.
